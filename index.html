Joey "CoCo" Diaz

Joey "CoCo" Diaz (born February 19, 1963) is an American stand up comedian.

He was born in Cuba, and raised in North Bergen, New Jersey. He lost his father at age 3 and then his mother at age 15. Various families in North Bergen gave their assistance to Joey by offering their hospitality to him. Joey says a total of 20 Italian and Irish families attempted to assist him. He explains that their sense of humor greatly influenced his own career, which had its earliest beginnings as a student in North Bergen High School.

Joey's struggle as an a young man who lost his parents early in life led to him living a life of frequent criminal activity. He often comitted robberies and sold drugs. When the heat became too much, he left North Bergen for the University of Colorado at Boulder on June 1, 1985, but his academic life was interrupted when he was arrested for kidnapping and aggravated robbery, for which he served time in prison. His standup comedy career began in prison, where he gained popularity by improvising material in front of Thursday night Movie Night audiences after the projector malfunctioned.

After he was released from prison and frequenting open mics in Denver and Seattle, Joey eventually moved to Los Angeles, where he began performing in front of open mic audiences. After his career spread to other venues, he accepted an offer from a CBS talent scout to work on a television pilot. Although the series was not purchased, the visibility it gave him helped his career, garnering him appearances on the television show Law & Order and in films including Spider-Man 2 and the 2004 film Taxi. His most visible role was in the 2005 film The Longest Yard and as a prison convict named Joey the Candy Bar Criminal, a part he played in four 2007 episodes of My Name is Earl, and which he obtained on the basis of his own past in prison.

He became a fan favorite on Joe Rogan's popular podcast The Joe Rogan Experience. Listeners really enjoyed listening to stories of his crazy childhood in North Bergen and all his drug-fueled misadventures.

In 2010 he began co-hosting Beauty and Da Beast with his friend and comedian Felicia Michaels. It ended in 2012 after releasing over one hundred episodes.
